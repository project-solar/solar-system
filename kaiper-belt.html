Kuiper Belt

  • The kuiper belt also known as Edge worth - kuiper belt is like a circular disc surrounding the solar system. It starts from Neptune (30 AU) and extends up-to 50 AU from the sun.
  • Kuiper belt is a very important part of the solar system as it servers as a reservoir for the short period comets.
  • The kuiper belt also contains three main dwarf planets - Pluto, haumea and makemake. It is said that some of the moons such as Neptune's Triton and Saturn's phoebe and originated in this region.
  • The kuiper belt is similar to the asteroid belt except this belt is 20 times wider and 20-200 times as massive.
  • This is named after a Dutch astronomer, Gerard kuiper who in 1951 suggested that comets may have suggested in this region.
  • Interesting facts - The Pluto is the largest object in the kuiper belt. This is what made it be reclassified into a dwarf planet in 2006.
